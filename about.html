Our Story

"I'm sorry."

Those were the words that came out too many times. That stops here.

Nesta loves the swing.

The moment he gets on it, something shifts. His body settles. His face changes. You can see him arrive, properly arrive, in a way that does not happen everywhere. For a child who is autistic, non verbal, and navigating the world through a rare genetic condition, that swing is not just fun. It is regulation. It is joy. It is safety.

But for a long time, finding a place where Nesta could swing freely, be genuinely understood, and not become a problem for staff who were simply not equipped, felt almost impossible in Nairobi.

"We were asked to leave within thirty minutes. Not because Nesta had done anything wrong. But because the attendant had no idea how to support a non verbal child who was disregulated. He was not a problem to be managed. He was a child who needed to be understood."

At another play area, Nesta found the corner of the room. The quietest spot he could locate, as far from the noise and the crowd as possible. He stayed there. That image stayed with us too. Not because it was sad but because it was so telling. He was coping. The space had not been designed for him.

And he was not alone. At that same facility there was another neurodivergent child with a nanny who was clearly overwhelmed and undertrained. No one was helping. No one knew how. Two families, in the same room, completely invisible to the space around them.

The sensory swing area and creative table at Neuronest, designed with occupational therapists from day one.

A family came together to build what did not exist

What happened next was not a business decision. It was a family decision. Nesta's father, his aunt, his uncle, and a beloved aunt who is family in every sense that matters came together around one shared belief: build the space Nesta deserved, and build it so well that no other family ever has to keep apologising.

For a full year before we opened our doors, we consulted occupational therapists, speech therapists, behavioural specialists, and early childhood development experts. We asked what neurodivergent children genuinely need in a play space. We listened to what had failed other families. We built Neuronest around those answers, one deliberate detail at a time.

One of our therapy suites at Neuronest, available for occupational therapy, speech therapy and more.

The lighting levels. The acoustic treatment. The sensory swing. The quiet corner. The therapy suites on site. The trained staff on the floor at all times. None of it was accidental.

We understand the parent's journey

We know what it is like to carry the weight of a system that was not built for your child. At Neuronest, you get to decide how you spend your time. Stay right beside your child if that is what you both need. Sit nearby and watch as they explore. Or, if you are ready, step back completely while our professionals take care. Every option is valid. We do not judge any of it.

"We want every parent who walks through that door to feel like they have arrived. Like someone finally knows what they have been through. And like their child is safe here, truly safe."

What we built inside Neuronest

Sensory swings, trapeze bars and zip line for vestibular input and movement
Crash pit and climbing wall for safe proprioceptive exploration
Quiet corner for children who need to regulate away from the group
Sensory wall with tactile panels, mirrors and light elements
Creative play pods for art, imagination and connection
Learning room with structured play tools and sensory activities
Therapy suites for occupational therapy, speech therapy and more
ECD trained play attendants on the floor at all times
Visual schedules and clear signage throughout the space
Parent waiting area so you are always close

Neuronest was built by a family who lived the gap, shaped by professionals who understood the need, created for every family who had been told their child was too much for the world's existing spaces.

Nesta has his swing now. And every child who walks through our door deserves the same.

This is only the beginning

The play area is where we start. But our vision goes further. We are building a full ecosystem of support for neurodivergent children and their families, including access for families who cannot afford market rates. Because need does not stop at a certain income level.

And beyond the children we serve directly, we believe Neuronest has a role to play in changing how this society sees neurodivergent people altogether. There is a saying that people fear what they do not understand. When typical children grow up playing alongside neurodivergent children, when they see and experience difference from an early age, that fear does not take root. We do not want the next generation of neurodivergent children to grow up in a world that was not built for them. That work starts here.

Welcome to Neuronest. Where every child is welcome, every family is understood, and no one ever has to say "I'm sorry" again.
